Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is a type of online scam where people are tricked into sending money or sharing their banking details. This usually happens through fake phone calls, messages, or links that look like they are coming from banks, payment apps, or customer support.

A common situation is when a student receives a call from someone pretending to be a bank official. The caller says there is a problem with the account and asks for verification. The student is then sent a payment request or a link. Thinking it is genuine, the student enters their UPI PIN or approves the request. Instead of receiving money, the amount gets deducted from their account. In some cases, scammers also send QR codes or ask users to install unknown apps, which can further compromise their device.

College students are often targeted because they regularly use UPI apps like Google Pay, and PhonePe but may not be fully aware of these scams. Personally, I have also seen messages and calls like this, which shows how common these frauds are.

The impact can be serious, including loss of money, stress, and misuse of personal information. In many cases, it is difficult to recover the money, especially if the fraud is not reported quickly.
